 Q1. How do we add or update a nominee across mutual fund folios and PMS
 accounts?
 Nominee Addition & Updation

What is Nominee Addition/Updation?
A nominee is a person designated to receive the mutual fund units or PMS account assets in the event of the
primary investor's death. Adding or updating a nominee is a critical estate-planning step. SEBI has mandated
that all investors must either register a nominee or opt out explicitly. Up to 3 nominees can be registered per
folio with defined percentage allocations totalling 100%.

Part A — MF Folio Nominee Update via MF Central (Online)
 COVERAGE: MF Central covers folios at both CAMS and KFintech RTAs. One request updates all eligible folios
 under the investor's PAN.

Step 1: Login to MF Central
Visit mfcentral.com → Click Login → Enter PAN or registered mobile number → Enter Date of Birth →
Validate OTP sent to registered mobile and email → Set MPIN on first login for faster future access.
Step 2: Navigate to Nominee Update
From the dashboard, click Service Requests in the sidebar → Select Update Nominee under the Account
Management section.
Step 3: Select Folios
MF Central displays all folios mapped to the PAN. Select the folios where nominee is to be added or updated.
You can select all folios at once or choose specific ones.
Step 4: Enter Nominee Details
For each nominee (up to 3), enter:
     • Full name as per PAN/Aadhaar
     • Relationship with the investor (spouse, child, parent, etc.)
     • Date of birth (if the nominee is a minor — guardian details also required)
     • Percentage allocation (all nominees must total 100%)
     • Address of the nominee
Step 5: Authenticate and Submit
Accept the declaration and consent on screen → OTP is sent to registered mobile and email → Enter OTP →
Click Submit. Do not close the page while the request is being processed.
Step 6: Save Acknowledgement

A Service Request Number (SRN) is generated and displayed. The same SRN is sent via SMS and email. Track
status under Service Requests → Track Status. Typical processing: 3–7 working days.

Part B — MF Folio Nominee Update via Individual AMC (Physical)
Step 1: Download Form
Download the Nominee Registration/Change form from the respective AMC's website or CAMS/KFintech
investor service centre.
Step 2: Fill the Form
Fill in folio number, investor name, nominee name, relationship, percentage allocation, and nominee's
address.
Step 3: Witness Signature
The form requires the investor's signature and one witness signature (the witness must not be the nominee).
Step 4: Submit
Submit the completed form with self-attested PAN and Aadhaar to the nearest AMC/RTA service centre or via
the RM.

Part C — PMS Account Nominee Update
 IMPORTANT: PMS nominee updates are handled directly with the PMS provider and may require submission of
 forms to the custody/registrar.

Step 1: Obtain Form
Request the Nominee Modification form from the PMS provider's operations team via your Guardian Capital
Relationship Manager.
Step 2: Complete Documentation
Fill in the form with:
      • Client account number / PMS account ID
      • Current nominee details (if updating)
      • New nominee full name, relationship, date of birth, address
      • Percentage allocation (if multiple nominees)
      • Nominee's PAN (recommended)
Step 3: Attach Supporting Documents
Self-attested PAN card of the investor; KYC proof of nominee (PAN or Aadhaar).
Step 4: Submit via RM
Submit to the Guardian Capital RM who will forward to the PMS provider's operations team.
Step 5: Processing Time
Nominee updates for PMS are processed in 5–10 working days. Confirmation is sent by the PMS provider.

 Q2. Can we update bank details through MF Central, and apply the change across
 all folios?
 Updating Bank Details via MF Central

Short Answer
Yes. Bank details can be updated through MF Central, and the request can be applied across all folios linked
to the investor's PAN in a single submission — subject to the conditions explained below.

 AT A GLANCE MF Central (mfcentral.com) is the joint CAMS + KFintech investor-service portal. Bank Mandate
 Change is a free, OTP-authenticated service that pushes the new bank account to every folio under the PAN
 serviced by CAMS or KFintech.

Step-by-Step Process
Operational checklist — share with client or do on their behalf via screen-share
 ▪ Go to mfcentral.com → click 'Sign In' → enter PAN + DOB.

  ▪ Complete OTP authentication — OTP is sent to BOTH registered mobile and registered email. If either
    OTP does not arrive, fix KYC contact details first (see Q1 in the repository).
  ▪ Navigate: Service Request → Non-Financial → Bank Mandate Registration / Change.
  ▪ Select scope: tick 'Update across all folios' for PAN-level change, OR select specific folios from the list.
  ▪ Enter new bank details: Account Number (re-enter to confirm), IFSC code, Account Type
    (Savings/Current/NRE/NRO), Branch (auto-fetched from IFSC).
  ▪ Upload cancelled cheque or passbook/statement image (PDF/JPG, < 2 MB). Name on the document must
    match the first holder's name on the folio.
  ▪ Review the summary screen → confirm with final OTP.
  ▪ Note the Service Request Number (SRN) shown on the acknowledgement screen. Save / share with the
    client.
  ▪ Track status: Service Requests → Track SR on MF Central. Confirmation email/SMS from each AMC arrives
    over the next 7–10 working days.
  CRITICAL If the new bank account name does not exactly match the first holder's name on the folio, MF
  Central will reject the request. For married women whose folio still has the maiden name, first complete a KRA
  name change (see KYC repository) before the bank update.

Why MF Central Is the Preferred Route
  ▪ One submission updates all eligible folios — eliminates AMC-by-AMC paperwork.
  ▪ Fully digital — no courier, no branch visit, no IPV in most cases.
  ▪ Free of cost and available 24×7.
  ▪ SRN-based tracking gives a single reference for follow-up across all AMCs.
  ▪ Audit trail (OTP + document upload) is retained on the portal — useful for compliance.
  TEAM NOTE For HNI clients with folios spread across 8–15+ AMCs, MF Central is the fastest and cleanest
  route. Fall back to AMC-level bank mandate forms ONLY for the exclusions listed above. Always confirm new
  bank details with the client over a recorded call before submitting on their behalf.

  Q3. Can we update contact details and the mobile number across all folios
  through MF Central or MFU?
  Updating Contact Details & Mobile Number

Short Answer
Yes. Both MF Central and MF Utility (MFU) allow an investor to update their mobile number and email ID,
and to apply the change across all folios linked to their PAN in a single request. The two platforms differ in
how they group folios — MF Central works at PAN level, while MFU works through the investor's CAN
(Common Account Number).

 AT A GLANCE MF Central (CAMS + KFintech) updates contact details across all folios under the PAN. MFU
 updates across all folios mapped to the investor's CAN. Either route is digital, OTP-authenticated, and free.

Process A — Via MF Central
Updates contact details across all folios under the PAN
 ▪ Go to mfcentral.com → Sign In → enter PAN + DOB.
 ▪ Authenticate via OTP sent to the CURRENT registered mobile and email. (If the old contacts are no longer
   accessible, use the offline KYC route instead — see note below.)
 ▪ Navigate: Service Request → Non-Financial → Update Email / Mobile.
 ▪ Enter the new mobile number and/or new email ID.
 ▪ Select relationship type for the contact (Self / Spouse / Dependent etc. — as per SEBI norms).
 ▪ Verify the new mobile and email via fresh OTP sent to the NEW contacts.
 ▪ Confirm submission → note the Service Request Number (SRN).
 ▪ Change reflects across all folios under the PAN in T+2 to T+7 working days; confirmation sent to the new
   contacts.

Process B — Via MF Utility (MFU)
Updates contact details across all folios mapped to the CAN
 ▪ Go to mfuonline.com → log in with CAN + password (or register if first-time).
 ▪ Authenticate via OTP sent to the registered mobile and email.

 ▪ Navigate: Profile / CAN Details → Modify CAN → Contact Details.
 ▪ Update the mobile number and/or email ID at the CAN level.
 ▪ Submit the CAN modification request → OTP verification on the new contacts.
 ▪ MFU processes the change and pushes it to ALL folios mapped to that CAN automatically.
 ▪ Acknowledgement and CAN modification reference are generated; change reflects in T+2 to T+5 working
   days.
 KEY DIFFERENCE On MFU, the contact details sit on the CAN itself — so updating the CAN automatically
 cascades to every folio transacted through that CAN. On MF Central, the update is applied PAN-wide across all
 serviced folios. Both achieve the 'all folios' outcome.

Important Conditions & Limitations
 ▪ Access to current contacts: OTP-based online update requires the OLD registered mobile/email to be
   reachable. If both are lost/inaccessible, the investor must use the offline KRA / AMC contact-change
   route (physical form + IPV).
 ▪ KYC contact vs. folio contact: MF Central / MFU update the FOLIO-level contact. The KRA KYC record
   contact is separate — for full alignment, also complete the KRA contact update and OTP validation at
   validate.cvlindia.com.
 ▪ Folios outside the platform's network: MF Central covers CAMS/KFin-serviced AMCs; MFU covers MFU-
   participant AMCs. A folio in an AMC outside the chosen platform's network must be updated directly
   with that AMC.
 ▪ Demat-held units: contact details are governed by the demat account; change them via the Depository
   Participant (DP), not MF Central/MFU.
 ▪ Third-party contact restriction: SEBI norms require the contact to belong to the investor (or a permitted
   family relationship). Declare the relationship correctly.
 ▪ Minor folios: mobile/email is that of the guardian until the minor attains majority.
 IMPORTANT Updating mobile/email on MF Central or MFU does NOT by itself mark the KRA KYC as
 'Validated'. After the folio-level update, complete OTP validation on the CVL KRA portal (validate.cvlindia.com)
 to achieve / retain KYC Validated status.

  Q4. Can we update an investor's name on MFU and across all folios?
  Updating the Name on MFU

Short Answer
No — a name change cannot be done online on MFU. It must be submitted as a physical (offline) request: the
MFU Name Change Request Form / KYC Modification Form, lodged at an MFU POS location or routed through
the advisor. The change takes effect only after MFU/AMC verification and confirmation. The online CAN-
modification screens do NOT allow editing the name.

  WHY OFFLINE-ONLY Name is a PAN-linked, high-scrutiny field tied to the Income Tax record and the KRA KYC.
  Platforms do not allow self-editing it online — it must be document-verified. MFU therefore requires a signed
  physical form plus proof, and validates it against PAN/KRA before applying it.

How the Name Updates Across All Folios
There are two layers — together they produce the all-folios result:
1. CAN level (MFU)
The name sits on the CAN. Once the physical request is verified, MFU updates the CAN name, which reflects
across all folios mapped to / transacted through that CAN.
2. PAN / KRA level (master record)
The KYC name change cascades to every folio linked to that PAN across all AMCs — including folios not
routed through the CAN. This is the true 'all folios' mechanism.

 NOT COVERED AUTOMATICALLY Folios in AMCs outside the MFU network update via the KRA cascade or
 directly at that AMC/RTA. Demat-held units are handled by the Depository Participant (DP), not MFU. Any folio
 with a differing holder-name structure needs AMC-level handling.

Step-by-Step Process
 ▪ Update the name on PAN first (NSDL / UTIITSL) and wait for the new PAN card.
 ▪ Download the Name Change Request Form / KYC Modification Form from the MFU Form Center
   (mfuonline.com).
 ▪ Fill in the CAN, the old name, and the new name.

  ▪ Have ALL holders sign the form.
  ▪ Attach: new PAN copy, new bank proof (cancelled cheque / statement with the new name), and the
    scenario document (marriage certificate / gazette / court order).
  ▪ Submit physically at an MFU POS location, or hand to the advisor for lodging.
  ▪ MFU / AMC verify and confirm → CAN name is updated → cascades to mapped folios. The KRA leg
    propagates the name to all PAN-linked folios.

Bottom Line
  ▪ Cannot be done online on MFU.
  ▪ Physical MFU Name Change / KYC Modification Form — all holders signing.
  ▪ PAN must already show the new name (new PAN copy mandatory).
  ▪ 'All folios' happens through the CAN cascade (MFU-mapped folios) AND the KRA name change (all PAN-
     linked folios, industry-wide).
  TEAM NOTE Always update PAN first, then lodge the physical MFU form with full proofs. Confirm the exact
  form name, whether new-bank-proof is strictly required for a pure name change, and current timelines against
  the mfuonline.com Form Center before client-facing use. After processing, verify the KRA record at
  validate.cvlindia.com.

  Q5. Can we change the second holder's name across all folios?
  Changing the Second Holder's Name

Short Answer
Yes — but only as a name CORRECTION for the same person, not as a substitution of one person for another.
A genuine name change/correction of the existing second (joint) holder — spelling correction, marriage,
divorce, or legal name change — can be done, and because it is keyed to that holder's PAN, it propagates to
all folios where that person appears as a holder. Like the first-holder case, it is a physical (offline) process, not
an online one.

 KEY RULE A 'name change' can only fix the name of the existing individual. Changing WHO the second holder
 is, is a change of holding pattern — which mutual funds do not allow except via transmission (death) or by
 redeeming and reinvesting in a new folio.

Pre-conditions for a Valid Second-Holder Name Correction
 ▪ The second holder's PAN must be updated first (NSDL / UTIITSL) — the new PAN showing the corrected
   name is mandatory.
 ▪ The second holder must be KYC compliant in their own right (own PAN, own KYC record).
 ▪ The new name must exactly match the second holder's updated PAN / KRA record.

How It Applies Across All Folios
 ▪ The second holder's name is linked to THEIR OWN PAN and KRA KYC. A KRA name change for that PAN
   cascades to every folio where that person is a holder — whether they sit as first or second holder —
   across all AMCs.
 ▪ On MFU, if the second holder is part of the CAN, the verified update reflects across all folios mapped to
   that CAN.
 ▪ Folios in AMCs outside the MFU / CAMS-KFin network, or demat-held units, update via the KRA cascade
   or must be handled directly with the AMC / Depository Participant.

Documents Required
 ▪ Name Change / KYC Modification Form — signed by ALL holders (the second holder must sign).
 ▪ Self-attested copy of the second holder's new PAN (corrected name).
 ▪ Scenario document: Marriage Certificate (marriage) / Court Order or Divorce Decree (divorce) / Gazette
   Notification or Court Order (legal change) / updated PAN, Aadhaar or passport (spelling correction).
 ▪ Where required by the AMC/platform, bank proof matching the folio's linked account.

Step-by-Step Process
 ▪ Update the second holder's PAN first; ensure their KYC reflects the new name.
 ▪ Lodge the physical Name Change / KYC Modification Form (MFU POS or AMC / RTA), signed by all holders.
 ▪ Attach the second holder's new PAN copy + the scenario document.
 ▪ After MFU / AMC / KRA verification, the corrected name propagates across all folios where that PAN is a
   holder.

Bottom Line
  ▪ Correcting the EXISTING second holder's name — yes, physical process, cascades across all folios via that
    holder's PAN / KRA.
  ▪ Swapping in a DIFFERENT second holder via 'name change' — not permitted; needs redemption + new
    folio, or transmission if a holder is deceased.

  Q7. Can a minor investor's status be changed to major (adult) online?
  Minor to Major Conversion

Short Answer
Largely no — it is predominantly an offline, forms-based process. When a minor turns 18, the folio is FROZEN
for all transactions (the guardian can no longer operate it) until the status is converted to 'major'. That
conversion requires the now-major's own KYC, own PAN, a new bank mandate in their own name, signature
attestation, and a photograph — all of which need document submission. There is no simple paperless self-
service for this across AMCs.

  KEY FACT On the minor's 18th birthday the folio is frozen. No purchases, redemptions, SIPs or switches are
  allowed until the Minor Attaining Majority (MAM) status change is processed and the folio is unfrozen.

Can We Do It on MF Central?
Only partially / not reliably as a fully-online action. MF Central is built around the EXISTING folio holder's PAN
+ OTP; a MAM case is a change of the operating individual (guardian -> the child as an adult), which is why it
sits outside the normal paperless flow.
  ▪ What MF Central MAY let you do: raise / track a service request and download the relevant form.
  ▪ What it does NOT do: complete the status change without the AMC's MAM form + fresh KYC + new bank
    proof + attestation being submitted (uploaded or physical) to the AMC / RTA (CAMS / KFintech).
  ▪ Treat this as a form-based process routed through the AMC / RTA, not a one-click online change.

 VERIFY ON LIVE FLOW MF Central periodically adds service types. Check the current 'Service Request' menu
 for a 'Minor Attaining Majority / Change of Status' option before assuming it is fully online — but plan for form
 + document submission either way.

  ▪ Processing typically takes a few working days; once done, the folio is UNFROZEN and operable by the

  ▪ Reliable route is offline / forms to the AMC or RTA (CAMS / KFintech).
  ▪ Forms: AMC website / CAMS / KFintech / MF Central / AMFI.

  Q9. Can the mode of holding be changed from Joint to Anyone or Survivor across
  all folios?
  Changing the Mode of Holding

Short Answer
No — not online, and on existing folios it is generally not permitted at all. The mode of holding is fixed at the
time the folio is created, and most AMCs do not allow it to be changed afterwards. Where an AMC does
permit it, it is a physical request signed by ALL joint holders, processed folio-by-folio at the AMC/RTA — there
is no single 'all folios' online toggle on MF Central or MFU.

Why It's Restricted
Changing Joint -> Anyone or Survivor materially changes each holder's transaction rights:

 Mode                                                             Who can transact

 Joint                                                            ALL holders must sign every transaction.

 Anyone or Survivor (AoS)                                         ANY ONE holder can transact alone; on death the
                                                                  survivor continues.

Going from Joint to AoS means a single holder can suddenly redeem / operate alone — so it reduces the
control of the other holders. Because of this, AMCs either disallow the change on an existing folio or require
the explicit consent (signatures) of every holder, with verification — which rules out a self-service online
action.

The Realistic Options

                                                                                         reinvest in a NEW folio opened with

                                                                                         capital-gains tax).

  DEFAULT APPROACH Most teams default to the redeem-and-reinvest route because many AMCs simply will
  not alter the mode of holding on an existing folio.

"Across All Folios"?
  ▪ There is no PAN-level or CAN-level toggle that flips mode of holding across all folios at once.
  ▪ Mode of holding is folio-specific — each folio needs its own request (where the AMC allows it), or its own

  ▪ MF Central / MFU do NOT expose this as a bulk online change.

  ▪ Submit the AMC's change request / service form for that folio.
  ▪ All joint holders sign (mandatory — consent of every holder).
  ▪ Provide signature attestation (banker / as the AMC requires).
  ▪ Submit at the AMC branch / CAMS / KFintech — repeat per folio.

  Q16. What is the transmission process for securities, MF units, and bonds held in
  demat mode?
  Transmission of Demat-Held Units & Bonds

The Key Principle
When shares, bonds AND mutual fund units are held in demat mode, on the holder's death everything in that
demat account transmits through the Depository Participant (DP) / depository (NSDL or CDSL) — NOT
through the AMC / RTA / MF Central. The whole account moves as one. (MF units bought in non-demat /
folio mode follow the RTA route in Q6 instead.)

  IF PURCHASED IN DEMAT MODE Do NOT file an MF transmission with CAMS / KFintech / MF Central — file a
  demat transmission with the DP. The MF units transmit along with the shares and bonds.

Which Route Applies — Depends on How the Account Was Held
 Situation                                                       Transmission goes to

 Joint demat account                                       The surviving holder(s)

 Sole holder, nominee registered                           The registered nominee

 Sole holder, NO nominee                                   The legal heirs

Steps (with the DP)
 ▪ Claimant intimates the DP of the death and obtains the Transmission Request Form (TRF).
 ▪ The claimant must have their OWN active demat account (open one if needed) — securities move from
   the deceased's demat into the claimant's demat.
 ▪ Submit the TRF + notarised / attested death certificate + claimant's demat details (Client Master Report).
 ▪ DP verifies and forwards the request to the depository (NSDL / CDSL).
 ▪ Securities (shares + bonds + MF units) are transmitted to the claimant's demat account — depository

 ▪ Once in the claimant's demat, MF units can be held or redeemed via the broker / DP; shares and bonds

Sole holder, NO nomination (value-scaled)

 ▪ Affidavit, Indemnity Bond, and NOC from other legal heirs.
 ▪ Up to the depository threshold (commonly Rs 5 lakh per account): the above suffice + relationship proof.
 ▪ Above the threshold: Succession Certificate / Probate of Will / Letter of Administration (or, where
   accepted, a registered Will / Legal Heirship Certificate).

Bottom Line
 ▪ Demat-held shares, bonds and MF units all transmit via the DP / depository, into the claimant's demat

 ▪ Do NOT route demat-mode MF units through MF Central / CAMS / KFintech — that's only for folio (non-

 ▪ Joint -> survivor; nominee -> nominee; none -> legal heirs (value-scaled documents).
 ▪ The claimant needs their own demat account to receive the securities.

  Q17. What is an ARN transfer, and how is it done?
  ARN Transfer (Change of Distributor)

What Is an ARN Transfer?
ARN stands for AMFI Registration Number — the unique code of a mutual fund distributor / broker. An ARN
Transfer (also called Change of Distributor / Change of Broker) means moving an investor's existing mutual
fund folios from one distributor's ARN to another distributor's ARN. It applies to REGULAR plan investments
(which carry an ARN). The investor's units, purchase value and holding stay the same — only the servicing
distributor code changes.

  GOOD TO KNOW No NOC from the existing broker is required — an investor can freely move their folios to a
  new distributor. Existing investments are not redeemed or disturbed; only the ARN mapping changes.

Client Process Flow — Regular (ARN) Transfer
Follow these steps in order:
  ▪ CHECK THE CAS & EXISTING BROKER: First pull the investor's CAS (Consolidated Account Statement) and
    confirm the existing broker / ARN, and that the holding is a REGULAR (ARN-linked) plan. (If it is a Direct
    plan, see the Direct / RIA sections below.)
  ▪ CHECK FOR ACTIVE SIP / STP / SWP: See whether any SIP, STP or SWP is running on the folios. If YES,
    these must be CANCELLED first — systematic registrations stay mapped to the old ARN and do not carry
    over automatically. Re-register them under the new broker after the transfer if the investor wishes to
    continue.
  ▪ FILL THE COMMON CAMS FORM: Complete the common Change-of-Broker / ARN-transfer form (CAMS),
    entering the MF folio details to be transferred to the new distributor.
  ▪ NEW BROKER SIGN & STAMP: The NEW distributor signs and affixes their stamp (with ARN and EUIN) on
    the form, along with the investor's signature.
  ▪ SUBMIT THE FORM: Submit the completed form at the RTA / AMC (CAMS / KFintech) service point.
  ▪ PROCESSING: The ARN transfer is processed in approximately T+11 working days, after which the folios
    reflect the new distributor's ARN.
  IMPORTANT Cancel any active SIP / STP / SWP BEFORE submitting the transfer. If not cancelled, the
  systematic transactions continue under the OLD ARN and will not move to the new broker.

  Q18. Can physical MF units be transferred from one individual to another — even
  as a gift to a family member?
  Transferring Physical MF Units

Short Answer
No — mutual fund units held in physical / non-demat (Statement of Account) form cannot be transferred
from one living individual to another. MF units in folio form are non-transferable inter-se. Ownership can
only change by transmission (on death) — not by gift or transfer between living persons.

Why
Unlike shares, MF units in folio (SOA) mode are NOT freely transferable. There is no 'change of owner' facility
on a folio. The investor on record stays the owner until:
  ▪ Transmission — units pass to the nominee / surviving holder / legal heir on the holder's death, or
  ▪ Redemption — the holder sells the units; the money can then be given to another person, who invests
    fresh in their own name (this is a sale -> exit load / capital-gains tax may apply).

Not Even as a Gift to a Family Member?
Correct — not even as a gift, if the units are in physical / non-demat (folio) form.
Why a gift doesn't work for physical units
  ▪ A folio has no 'gift' or 're-titling' facility — there is simply no mechanism to change the unit-holder on an
    existing physical folio while the holder is alive.
  ▪ 'Gifting' securities requires the units to move from one account to another. Folio (SOA) units don't sit in a
     transferable account, so there is nowhere to move them. Demat units do — which is the only reason
     gifting works there.
  ANSWER Physical MF units cannot be gifted to a family member. The practical route is to redeem and gift the
  money, or to gift only if the units are held in demat.

The One Exception — Demat-Held Units
If the MF units are held in demat mode, they CAN be transferred / gifted to another individual via an off-
market transfer through the Depository Participant (DP) — using a Delivery Instruction Slip (DIS), just like
shares (e.g., a gift to a family member). This is NOT possible for physical / folio units.

  TAX NOTE A demat gift transfer may carry clubbing-of-income / gift-tax considerations for the giver and the
  recipient — check before proceeding.

  Q19. Can demat MF units be transferred from one person to another? If so, what
  is the process?
  Transferring Demat MF Units

Short Answer
Yes. MF units held in demat form can be transferred from one demat account to another through an off-
market transfer via the Depository Participant (DP) — exactly like transferring shares. (This is the demat-only
exception noted in Q18; folio / physical units cannot be transferred this way.)

The Process
 ▪ Both parties must have active demat accounts — the transferor (giver) and the transferee (receiver). If
   the receiver has none, they open one first.
 ▪ Identify the units — note the ISIN of each MF scheme and the quantity (units) to transfer.
 ▪ Initiate the off-market transfer from the transferor's side, either: Physical — fill a Delivery Instruction Slip
    (DIS) and submit to the DP; or Online — CDSL 'easiest' or NSDL 'SPEED-e' (e-DIS), authorised with OTP /
    PIN.
 ▪ Enter the receiver's details — transferee's DP ID + Client ID (16-digit BO ID), the ISIN, quantity, the reason
   / transfer type (off-market / gift), and the consideration (mark as gift if no money changes hands;
   otherwise the value).
 ▪ Submit / authorise — the DP processes the instruction.
 ▪ Settlement — units move to the transferee's demat account, typically in 1-2 working days.

What the Client Needs to Keep Ready
Transferor (giver)
 ▪ Active demat account + DIS booklet (or registered access to CDSL easiest / NSDL SPEED-e for online).
 ▪ ISIN of each MF scheme and the number of units to transfer.
 ▪ Reason / transfer type (off-market / gift) and consideration amount (or 'gift').
 ▪ PAN and KYC-compliant account.
Transferee (receiver)
 ▪ Active demat account.
 ▪ DP ID + Client ID (BO ID) and a Client Master Report (CMR) copy.
 ▪ PAN and KYC-compliant account.
For a gift (recommended extras)
 ▪ A gift deed documenting the gift.
 ▪ Relationship proof between giver and receiver (for tax treatment).

Important Tax Note
 ▪ A gift to a 'relative' (as defined under the Income-tax Act — spouse, parents, children, siblings, etc.) is
   exempt in the receiver's hands.
 ▪ A gift to a non-relative above Rs 50,000 is taxable for the receiver.
 ▪ Clubbing of income can apply for gifts to spouse / minor child — flag this to the client.
 ▪ The transfer itself is NOT a redemption, so the giver's original cost and holding period carry over to the
   receiver for future capital-gains calculation.

Bottom Line
  ▪ Yes — demat MF units transfer via off-market transfer through the DP (DIS / CDSL easiest / NSDL SPEED-
    e).
  ▪ Keep ready: both demat accounts, ISIN + units, receiver's BO ID / CMR, PAN, reason / consideration, and a
    gift deed if gifting.
  ▪ Mind gift-tax and clubbing rules; cost & holding period carry over to the receiver.
  TEAM NOTE Exact e-DIS screens and timelines differ slightly between CDSL and NSDL, and DPs may ask for
  additional documentation for gifts — confirm with the specific DP before client-facing use.

  Q20. Can MF folios be converted between physical and demat form, and what is
  the process?
  Physical ⇄ Demat Conversion

Short Answer
Yes — both ways.
  ▪ Physical (folio / SOA) -> Demat = Dematerialisation, via a Conversion Request Form (CRF) through the DP.
  ▪ Demat -> Physical (folio / SOA) = Rematerialisation, via a Rematerialisation Request Form (RRF) through
    the DP.
Both are routed through the Depository Participant (DP), who forwards to the RTA / AMC (CAMS / KFintech).

The Golden Rule (Applies Both Ways)
  MUST MATCH The holding pattern must match exactly — the same holders, in the same order, with the same
  PAN on both the folio and the demat account. If they don't match, the conversion is rejected. All holders must
  sign the form.

A. Physical -> Demat (Dematerialisation)
Process
  ▪ Investor must have an active demat account with matching holding pattern.
  ▪ Obtain a Conversion Request Form (CRF) from the DP — a separate CRF for each AMC / scheme (each
    scheme has a unique ISIN).
  ▪ Fill the CRF: folio number, scheme name, ISIN, number of units.
  ▪ Attach the latest Statement of Account (SOA) of the folio.
  ▪ All holders sign the CRF.
  ▪ Submit CRF + SOA to the DP.
  ▪ DP verifies and forwards to the RTA (CAMS / KFintech).

  ▪ On confirmation, units are credited to the demat account — typically ~2-3 weeks.
Documents the client shares
Demat account details (DP ID + Client ID / CMR), latest SOA of the folio, filled & signed CRF (per scheme),
PAN, KYC-compliant account.

Process

  ▪ Fill the RRF: ISIN, scheme, number of units.
  ▪ All holders sign; submit to the DP.
  ▪ DP forwards to the RTA / AMC.

 Q21. How can a client's physical MF units held via GCIA be moved into GAM PMS
 (demat mode)?

Core Principle
This is NOT a sale / redemption — it is a change of form (physical -> demat) plus housing the units inside the
PMS. Because the beneficial owner stays the same client (same PAN) throughout, no capital gains are
triggered, and the original cost and holding period are preserved.

The Sequence (in Order)
Step 1 — Delete the GCIA RIA code from the folio (do this FIRST)
Remove / de-map GCIA's RIA / advisory code on the folio so that GCIA no longer receives any commission /

PMS.
Step 2 — Onboard the client to GAM PMS
Client signs the PMS agreement + Power of Attorney (POA), completes PMS KYC; GAM opens the PMS-
designated demat account in the client's name. Nothing can be moved until this PMS demat account exists.
Step 3 — Dematerialise the physical MF units into the PMS demat account
 ▪ Route A: demat directly into the GAM PMS demat account (Conversion Request Form + SOA to the DP), if
   holding pattern matches and the DP / PMS permits.
 ▪ Route B: demat into the client's own demat account first, then off-market transfer into the PMS demat
   account (in-kind contribution via DIS / e-DIS).

 GAM PMS onboarding -> PMS demat account opens. 3) Dematerialise the GCIA physical folios into the GAM

  ▪ Redemption proceeds are credited back to the SAME type of account (NRE -> repatriable, NRO -> non-
    repatriable).
  ▪ Capital-gains tax applies the same way regardless of account type; TDS is deducted on NRI mutual fund
    redemptions.
  ▪ Many NRIs maintain BOTH and choose per goal — repatriable investments via NRE, India-money
    investments via NRO.
  RULE OF THUMB Want the flexibility to take money back abroad freely -> invest via NRE (repatriable). Funds
  are from Indian sources or repatriation isn't needed -> NRO (non-repatriable).

Bottom Line
  ▪ NRE = foreign earnings, fully repatriable, tax-free interest.
  ▪ NRO = Indian income, repatriation capped at USD 1M/year, taxable interest.
  ▪ MF via NRE = repatriable folio; MF via NRO = non-repatriable folio.
  ▪ Proceeds return to the same account type; choose by repatriation need and source of funds.
  TEAM NOTE Confirm the investor's source of funds and repatriation intent before mapping the bank account
  to the folio. Repatriation limits and TDS rates are governed by FEMA / Income-tax rules and can change —
  verify current figures before advising.

Reference: RBI / FEMA NRI account rules (NRE / NRO) | Income-tax TDS on NRI MF redemptions | AMC NRI investment norms.

  Q2. Once an investor becomes an NRI, which account type — NRE or NRO —
  should their folios be updated to?
  Updating Folios on Becoming an NRI

Short Answer
NRO — on a non-repatriable basis. The existing folios were created while the investor was a resident, using
domestic / Indian funds, so those holdings are inherently non-repatriable. When the person becomes an NRI,
their resident savings account is redesignated as an NRO account, and the MF folios must be updated to link
that NRO account (folio status -> NRI, non-repatriable).

Why NRO and Not NRE
  ▪ NRE is only for FRESH investments made from foreign earnings on a repatriable basis. You cannot
    retroactively make old, resident-funded investments repatriable — so existing holdings can't be moved
    to NRE.
  ▪ The investments already made stay non-repatriable, which is exactly what an NRO account is for.

  ▪ Under FEMA, when a resident becomes an NRI, the existing resident savings account is converted /
    redesignated to NRO — and the folios simply follow that account.
  KEY POINT Existing resident-funded holdings -> NRO (non-repatriable). They cannot be mapped to NRE,
  because repatriability cannot be granted retroactively.

What Actually Needs to Be Done
  ▪ Redesignate the resident savings account -> NRO (the bank does this on status change).
  ▪ Update KYC residential status to NRI (KYC modification with IPV — see RI -> NRI process).
  ▪ Update the bank mandate to the NRO account across all MF folios (so redemptions credit to the NRO
    account).
  ▪ Existing units are now held on a non-repatriable (NRO) basis.

Going Forward (New Investments)
  ▪ The NRI can also open NRE-linked folios for fresh investments from foreign earnings if they want those
    repatriable.
  ▪ So a typical NRI ends up with: old folios -> NRO (non-repatriable), and optionally new folios -> NRE
    (repatriable).
 Holdings                                     Account                                     Basis

 Existing (made as resident)                  NRO                                         Non-repatriable

 New investments from foreign funds           NRE                                         Repatriable

Bottom Line
  ▪ Existing folios -> NRO (non-repatriable) — the correct update on becoming an NRI.
  ▪ Existing resident-funded holdings cannot go to NRE (no retroactive repatriability).
  ▪ Use NRE only for new repatriable investments from foreign funds.
  ▪ Redesignate the bank account to NRO, update KYC to NRI, and re-map the NRO account across all folios.
  TEAM NOTE Exact bank redesignation steps and whether a specific AMC needs a fresh folio for NRO mapping
  vs. updating the existing one can vary — confirm with the AMC / RTA before client-facing use. See also the RI ->
  NRI status-change documents and process.

Reference: RBI / FEMA NRI account rules (resident -> NRO redesignation) | KRA KYC RI -> NRI reclassification | AMC NRI folio
mapping.

  Q3. If the first holder is Resident Indian and the second holder is NRI, what is the
  folio's tax status?
  Folio Tax Status — RI First, NRI Second

Short Answer
The folio's tax / residential status follows the FIRST (primary) holder. So the folio is a RESIDENT (Resident
Indian) folio. The second holder being an NRI does NOT change it — the entire folio is operated, taxed, and
reported on the basis of the first holder's resident status.

 THE RULE A folio's residential / tax status is always set by the first / sole holder. Joint holders' status does not
 drive the folio's tax treatment.

Why
 ▪ In mutual funds, the residential and tax status of a folio is always determined by the first / sole holder —
    not the joint holders.
 ▪ First holder = Resident Indian => folio status = Resident, normal resident taxation applies (no NRI TDS on
   the folio), and redemption proceeds go to the first holder's resident bank account.
 ▪ The NRI second holder is just a joint holder — their status is recorded but does NOT drive the folio's tax
   treatment.

Points to Keep in Mind
 ▪ The second (NRI) holder's PAN and KYC must still be valid / compliant.
 ▪ FATCA / CRS: even though the folio is 'Resident', if the second holder is a foreign tax resident, the folio
   may still get captured in FATCA / CRS reporting because of that holder — declare correctly.
 ▪ If the FIRST holder later becomes an NRI, then the folio status changes to NRI (see Q2) — the trigger is
    always the first holder.
 ▪ A resident folio uses a resident bank mandate (first holder's account).

Quick Reference
 First Holder                            Second Holder                           Folio Tax Status

 Resident Indian                         NRI                                     Resident Indian

 NRI                                     Resident Indian                         NRI

 Resident Indian                         Resident Indian                         Resident Indian

 NRI                                     NRI                                     NRI

Bottom Line
 ▪ Folio tax status = Resident Indian (set by the first holder).
 ▪ NRI second holder does not change the folio's tax status.
 ▪ Resident taxation applies; proceeds to the resident first-holder account.
 ▪ Keep the NRI joint holder's PAN / KYC valid; mind FATCA / CRS reporting.
 TEAM NOTE The first-holder rule is standard AMC / RTA practice, but FATCA / CRS treatment of a folio with an
 NRI joint holder can have nuances — confirm with the AMC if reporting questions arise.

Reference: AMC / RTA folio status rules (first-holder basis) | Income-tax residential status & TDS | FATCA / CRS reporting.

  Updating Address Across All Folios

Q5. Updating Overseas & Indian Address for NRI Clients Across All Folios
Does updating the client's KYC fetch the change across all folios?

Short Answer — Yes, the KYC Update Cascades
You update the address once at the KYC (KRA) level, and it cascades across all folios linked to that PAN. MF
folios don't store the address independently — they fetch it from the central KYC record (KRA). So a single
KRA KYC address modification flows to every folio under that PAN across all AMCs; you do NOT update each
folio one by one.

  MENTAL MODEL 'Update the client's KYC and it fetches across all folios' is exactly right. The KRA record is the
  single source; folios sync from it.

How NRI Addresses Are Held
The KYC record has two address fields, and for NRIs both can be captured:

 ▪ Overseas address — the foreign residential address (correspondence address for an NRI).
 ▪ Indian address — the Indian correspondence / permanent address, if the client has one.
Updating either (or both) is done through the KYC modification (address change) — which then propagates to
all folios.

The Process
 ▪ Fill the KYC Change / Modification form — address section, entering the new overseas and / or Indian
   address.
 ▪ Attach address proof — Overseas: overseas utility bill / bank statement (< 3 months), or foreign driving
   licence / residence permit showing the address. Indian: Aadhaar / passport / Indian utility bill / bank
   statement (< 3 months).
 ▪ IPV is required for an address change — in person at an Indian intermediary, or via Indian Embassy /
    Consulate / notary attestation if the client is abroad.
 ▪ Submit to the KRA via CAMS / KFintech / AMC (or initiate on MF Central at PAN level with document
   upload, where supported).
 ▪ KRA updates the record -> it cascades to all folios linked to the PAN (allow a few working days for AMCs
   to refresh from KRA).

Does It Really Fetch Across All Folios Automatically?
 ▪ Yes — KRA is the central KYC repository; all AMCs / RTAs pull KYC data (including address) from KRA by
   PAN. One KRA update reflects across all folios.
 ▪ Caveat 1: there is a short cascade lag (a few days) before every AMC reflects it.
 ▪ Caveat 2: if a particular AMC holds a separate folio-level address that differs from KYC, that AMC may
   need its own update — but generally the KRA / KYC address governs.
 ▪ CKYC: the update also flows to the CKYC / CERSAI record within a few days.

Bottom Line
 ▪ Update address once at KRA (KYC modification) -> cascades to all folios under the PAN. No per-folio
   updates needed.
 ▪ Both overseas and Indian addresses can be updated together in the address section.
 ▪ Needs address proof (< 3 months) + IPV (embassy / consulate / notary attestation if abroad).

  ▪ Allow a few days for the cascade; CKYC also updates.

Attestation — Two Rules
Rule 1 — Who can attest (when attestation is needed)
When attestation is required, the overseas address-proof copies must be attested by one of the following:

  ▪ Indian Embassy / Consulate / High Commission in the country of residence.
  ▪ Notary Public (overseas).
  ▪ Authorised official of an overseas branch of a scheduled commercial bank registered in India (banker
    attestation), or a correspondent bank.
Rule 2 — When attestation is actually required
Overseas attestation is required ONLY if the client has NOT visited India in the last 6 months.
  ▪ Client has visited India in the last 6 months (or is currently in India) -> documents can be self-attested and
    verified in person (IPV) in India. No embassy / consulate / notary / banker attestation needed.
  ▪ Client has NOT visited India in the last 6 months (genuinely abroad, not coming) -> the overseas address
    proof copies must be attested as per Rule 1.

  KEY POINT Attestation is a fallback for when the client can't present themselves in India. Visited India within 6
  months => in-person self-attestation is enough; no India visit in 6 months => embassy / consulate / notary /
  Indian-bank overseas branch attestation required.

  ▪ Bills / statements must be < 3 months old.
  ▪ All copies self-attested by the client; add Rule-1 attestation only when Rule 2 requires it.
  ▪ Carry originals for IPV where in-person verification is required (address change needs IPV).

  Q9. Is it mandatory for all NRI investors to open a PIS account, and who actually
  needs one?
  Is a PIS Account Mandatory?

Short Answer
No — PIS is NOT mandatory for all NRI investors. A PIS (Portfolio Investment Scheme) account is required
only for an NRI who wants to buy / sell listed equity shares and convertible debentures on the stock exchange
(secondary market) on a REPATRIABLE basis (NRE-PIS). For mutual funds, bonds, IPOs, and the non-
repatriable (NRO) route, PIS is not needed.

Who Actually Needs PIS
Needs PIS (NRE-PIS): an NRI trading listed shares / convertible debentures on the secondary market (stock
exchange) on a repatriable basis — PIS tracks the NRI shareholding limits (5% individual / 10% aggregate of a
company).

 Mutual fund investments                                            No — MFs never need PIS

 Bonds                                                              No (generally)
 NCDs
 G-Secs

 IPO                                                                No — primary market is outside PIS
 primary-market subscriptions

 Secondary-market equity on NON-repatriable (NRO)                   No — RBI removed the PIS requirement for the NRO
 basis                                                              route

 Secondary-market equity on REPATRIABLE (NRE) basis                 Yes — NRE-PIS required

Key Points to Remember
  ▪ PIS is an equity / secondary-market tool, not a general NRI investment requirement.
  ▪ Only ONE PIS designation (one bank branch) is allowed for the repatriable route — you can't run multiple
    PIS accounts.
  ▪ The NRO (non-repatriable) route needs no PIS — those investments are treated like domestic ones.
  ▪ Mutual fund NRIs don't need PIS at all — a normal NRE / NRO account is enough.

  Folio 'Status Mismatch'

First, Understand the Flag in One Line
  An investor can hold only one residential status at a time. A 'Status Mismatch' means the same PAN has some
  folios tagged NRI and others tagged RI — so the records contradict each other and must be made consistent.

Why It Happened (Almost Always One Reason)
The investor's status changed (usually RI -> NRI) and the update was done on some folios / AMCs but not all
— the rest were left on the old status. The KRA record may also be out of sync. Nothing is 'wrong' with the
money; only the status tagging is inconsistent.

  solution. Everything below is just how to do that.

Step-by-Step

folio should show.
Step 2 — Fix the KRA KYC record first
KRA is the central source — folios sync their status from it. Correct the status there (KYC modification) and it
cascades to the AMCs.
Step 3 — Update the folios that are still wrong
At each AMC / RTA where a folio shows the old / wrong status, submit the residential-status update so all
folios converge to the correct one.
Step 4 — Match the bank account to the status

                                                          FATCA
                                                          CRS

Q16. Claiming Unclaimed MF Redemption Proceeds (MITRA & MF Central)
Tracing inactive / unclaimed folios and claiming the money for an NRI

The Two-Part Idea
  ▪ TRACE it — find the forgotten / unclaimed folio using MITRA.
  ▪ CLAIM it — update KYC and submit the claim via MF Central / the AMC-RTA, so the money is paid to the

  ▪ MITRA = Mutual Fund Investment Tracing and Retrieval Assistant — a SEBI platform (launched Feb 2025)
    built by the QRTAs CAMS + KFintech.
  ▪ It lets an investor do a PAN-based search to trace inactive and unclaimed folios (an 'inactive' folio = no

  ▪ It also facilitates the KYC update needed to recover the money.
  ▪ Access: via links on MF Central, AMC, AMFI, CAMS / KFintech, and SEBI websites.
  ROLE SPLIT MITRA is the tracing / discovery tool; the actual claim and payout is then processed through the
  AMC / RTA (and MF Central for the service request).

Step-by-Step for an NRI
Step 1 — Trace the folios (MITRA)
Open MITRA (via MF Central / AMFI / CAMS / KFintech / SEBI) and run a PAN-based search to list the client's
inactive / unclaimed folios across AMCs.
Step 2 — Update the NRI KYC (the usual blocker)
Bring KYC current: residential status (NRI), FATCA / CRS + TIN, and a valid NRO / NRE bank mandate.

Step 4 — Verification
The RTA / AMC verifies (signature / KYC; IPV may apply for NRIs not in India per the 6-month rule).
Step 5 — Payout
The unclaimed redemption / IDCW amount (with applicable return as per SEBI's unclaimed-amount rules) is
credited to the NRI's NRO / NRE account.

If the Original Investor Is Deceased
Unclaimed money in a deceased NRI's folio is claimed via transmission (nominee / legal heir) — trace with
MITRA, then follow the transmission process with the AMC / RTA.

Overview
Yes — bonds can be gifted to a US-citizen son or to another Resident Indian. Under the Income-tax Act, gifts

border gifting to a US-citizen NRI is governed by FEMA limits and creates US tax considerations (PFIC /
reporting). The route also depends on whether the bonds are in demat or physical form.

  SEBI RULE Direct transfer of physical market securities (e.g., listed corporate bonds) is not permitted — they
  must first be dematerialised before they can be gifted.

Scenario A — Gifting to a Resident Indian (RI)
Qualifying relatives: spouse, siblings, parents, lineal ascendants / descendants and their spouses.
If bonds are in DEMAT form (off-market transfer)
  ▪ Ensure the recipient has an active demat account.
  ▪ Fill the Delivery Instruction Slip (DIS) from your Depository Participant (DP).
  ▪ Enter the recipient's Client ID and DP ID.
  ▪ Mark the 'Reason for Transfer' strictly as 'Gift'.
  ▪ Submit the DIS to your broker / DP to execute the transfer.
Alternative: many brokers allow an OTP-verified online 'gifting' flow.
If bonds are in PHYSICAL form
  ▪ Government bonds / G-Secs: transferred via the RBI Public Debt Office (PDO) — sign the transfer form
    and submit with the certificates; valid only once the PDO updates its ledger.
  ▪ Corporate / PSU bonds: cannot be transferred physically — first dematerialise (DRF) into your own
    demat, then follow the demat steps above.

 RESIDENT TAX The gift is 100% tax-free in the recipient's hands (Section 56(2)(x)) for specified relatives. Note:
 RBI Floating Rate Savings Bonds are resident-only and cannot be held by NRIs.

Scenario B — Gifting to a US-Citizen Son (NRI / OCI)
Permitted, but with FEMA limits and US tax complications (PFIC). The son must be eligible to hold Indian
securities (OCI) with an NRO demat.
If bonds are in DEMAT form
 ▪ Your son opens an NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) demat account in India.
 ▪ Execute an off-market transfer using a DIS form to the son's NRO demat.
 ▪ FEMA limit: cross-border gifting of securities from a resident to an NRI relative is capped at USD 50,000
   equivalent per financial year (a cash gift, if bonds are liquidated and remitted, falls under the LRS limit of
   USD 250,000 per FY).
 ▪ Draft a formal Gift Deed on stamp paper to establish the non-commercial nature.
If bonds are in PHYSICAL form

  Q11. What is the transfer and transmission process for bonds?
  Transfer & Transmission of Bonds

These are two different things — transfer is voluntary and happens during the holder's lifetime; transmission
happens by operation of law on the holder's death.

A. Transfer (During the Holder's Lifetime)
Moving bonds from one person to another while alive — either a gift or a sale.
Demat-held bonds (the normal case)
  ▪ Done via an off-market transfer using a Delivery Instruction Slip (DIS).
  ▪ Enter the transferee's demat (DP ID + Client ID), the ISIN, quantity, and the reason — 'Gift' (Bond Q10) or
    a sale / transfer.
  ▪ A sale settles through the exchange / ICCL (Bond Q9); a gift moves with nil consideration + a gift deed.
Physical bonds
  ▪ Direct physical transfer of listed securities is not permitted (SEBI) — they must be dematerialised first
    (DRF), then transferred via the demat route.
  ▪ Government securities (G-Secs): physical transfer is processed through the RBI Public Debt Office (PDO)
    — valid only once the PDO updates its ledger.

B. Transmission (On the Holder's Death)
Passing the bonds to the rightful claimant after death — not a sale or gift.
Demat-held bonds -> through the Depository Participant (DP)

 Situation                                                        Bonds go to

 Joint demat account                                              The surviving holder(s)

 Sole holder, nominee registered                                  The registered nominee

 Sole holder, no nominee                                          The legal heirs

Process
  ▪ Claimant intimates the DP and obtains the Transmission Request Form (TRF).
  ▪ Claimant must have their own demat account (bonds move into it).
  ▪ Submit TRF + notarised death certificate + claimant's demat (CML) details.
  ▪ DP verifies and forwards to the depository (NSDL / CDSL).
  ▪ Bonds are transmitted to the claimant's demat (typically a few weeks).
Documents — sole holder, no nomination (value-scaled)
  ▪ TRF + death certificate + claimant demat details.
  ▪ Affidavit, Indemnity Bond, NOC from other heirs.
  ▪ Above the depository threshold (commonly Rs 5 lakh): Succession Certificate / Probate of Will / Letter of
    Administration (or a registered Will / legal heirship certificate where accepted).
Government securities (G-Secs / physical)
Transmission is processed through the RBI PDO with the death certificate + succession documents.

Key Points
  ▪ Transfer = lifetime (gift / sale, via DIS off-market or exchange); physical must be dematerialised first (G-
    Secs via RBI PDO).
  ▪ Transmission = on death, via the DP: joint -> survivor, nominee -> nominee, none -> legal heirs (value-
    scaled docs).
  ▪ In both cases, demat-held bonds move between demat accounts — the claimant / transferee needs their
     own demat.
  TEAM NOTE The value threshold and document set for no-nomination transmission are set by NSDL / CDSL

  Off-Market Transfer Between Demat Accounts

What It Is
An off-market transfer moves securities directly from one demat account to another — without an exchange
trade. It's used for gifts, transfers between your own accounts, family transfers, or moving holdings to a PMS
/ another broker. Two ways to execute it: a physical DIS or an electronic EDIS (e-DIS).

Information You Need (Both Methods)
 ▪ Target (transferee) demat: DP ID + Client ID — i.e., the 16-digit BO ID (CDSL) or DP ID + Client ID (NSDL).
 ▪ ISIN of each security (share / bond).
 ▪ Quantity.
 ▪ Reason / transfer type: off-market — Gift / transfer between own accounts / others.
 ▪ Consideration (amount, or 'nil / gift').
 ▪ Execution date.

Method 1 — Physical DIS (Delivery Instruction Slip)
 ▪ Obtain the DIS booklet from your DP (the source demat's DP).
 ▪ Fill in: target DP ID + Client ID, ISIN, quantity, reason, consideration, date.
 ▪ All holders sign the slip.
 ▪ Submit to the DP before the cut-off.
 ▪ DP processes — securities are debited from the source and credited to the target (usually T+1 / same
   day).

Method 2 — EDIS / e-DIS (Electronic, Online)
 ▪ CDSL: use 'easiest' (registered facility) or e-DIS with TPIN — add the beneficiary BO ID, select securities,
   and authorise with TPIN + OTP.
 ▪ NSDL: use 'SPEED-e' — submit the instruction online, authenticated by password / e-token + OTP.
 ▪ Flow: log in -> set up the transfer -> enter target BO ID, ISIN, quantity, reason -> authenticate (OTP / TPIN
   / PIN) -> submit. Settles typically T+1.

 Q3. What is KRA validation, and do all investors need to complete it?
 KRA Validation — What It Is & Who Needs It

 📋 Quick Answer: KRA Validation is the process by which KYC Registration Agencies (KRAs) verify an investor's
 identity details against official government databases — PAN, Aadhaar, and contact details (mobile + email via
 OTP). It is NOT required by all investors equally — only those with 'KYC Registered' or 'KYC On-Hold' status
 need to act. Investors who are already 'KYC Validated' need do nothing.

1. What is KRA Validation?
KRA Validation (also called KYC Validation) is a mandatory regulatory process introduced by SEBI under which
KYC Registration Agencies (KRAs) independently verify each investor's KYC record against official government
databases. The goal is to ensure that the identity and contact information on file at the KRA is accurate,
current, and government-verified.
It is different from the original KYC process (where investors submit identity documents for the first time).
KRA Validation is an ongoing compliance check that applies even to investors who completed their KYC years
ago.

2. The 5 Points KRAs Check During Validation
For a KYC record to reach 'Validated' status, all five of the following must be confirmed:
    1. PAN Verification — PAN confirmed as valid and active against the Income Tax Department database
    2. Name & Date of Birth Match — Name and DOB on KYC record matched with PAN/Aadhaar records
    3. Address Validation — Address on file matched with Aadhaar or other Officially Valid Document
    (OVD)
    4. Mobile Number Verification — Investor confirms mobile via OTP at the KRA portal
    5. Email ID Verification — Investor confirms email via OTP at the KRA portal
 Note: PAN–Aadhaar linkage is an additional check. If an investor's PAN is not linked to their Aadhaar, full 'KYC
 Validated' status may not be achievable until the linkage is completed on the Income Tax portal
 (www.incometax.gov.in).

3. The Three KYC Statuses (Effective April 1, 2024 — SEBI Circular)
As per SEBI's May 2024 circular, all investor KYC records are classified into one of three statuses.
Understanding your investor's status is the first step before deciding what action to take:

 KYC VALIDATED Who has this status? Investors whose KYC has been verified on all 5 points — PAN,

 name/DOB, address, mobile OTP, email OTP — and whose PAN is linked to Aadhaar.
 Transactions ALLOWED: ALL transactions permitted — new investments, redemptions, SIPs, switches,
 investing with any new AMC, NRI transactions.
 Transactions BLOCKED: None — no restrictions.
 Action required: NO ACTION REQUIRED. This is the highest compliance level.

 ⚠️ KYC REGISTERED (also shown as 'KYC Verified')
 Who has this status? Investors whose KYC was done with non-Aadhaar OVDs, or whose mobile/email is
 partially verified (only one of the two confirmed), or whose PAN–Aadhaar linkage is incomplete.
 Transactions ALLOWED: Existing SIPs, redemptions, and switches with AMCs already transacted with — these
 CONTINUE unaffected.
 Transactions BLOCKED: New investments with AMCs not previously transacted with requires fresh Aadhaar-
 based KYC or document resubmission.
 Action required: Should validate mobile + email via KRA portal to upgrade to 'KYC Validated'. Complete PAN–
 Aadhaar linkage if pending.

 KYC ON-HOLD Who has this status? Investors whose address validation failed, or BOTH mobile and email are
 unverified at the KRA. May also occur when KYC was done with non-OVD documents.
 Transactions ALLOWED: None — all transactions are BLOCKED.
 Transactions BLOCKED: ALL — new investments, redemptions, SIPs, switches blocked until KYC is remediated.
 Action required: URGENT: Must complete fresh KYC immediately through a SEBI-registered intermediary

5. June 2024 Amendment — What Changed?
SEBI issued a revision effective June 1, 2024 that relaxed certain On-Hold classifications:
 ▪ Investors previously 'On-Hold' only because ONE of their two contacts (mobile OR email) was verified (but
    not both) — MOVED to 'KYC Registered' status. They can now transact with existing AMCs while
    completing full validation.
 ▪ Investors previously 'On-Hold' because of PAN–Aadhaar non-compliance — MOVED to 'KYC Registered'
    status. They must still link PAN–Aadhaar but are no longer fully blocked.
 ▪ Investors with NEITHER mobile NOR email verified — remain 'On-Hold'. They must get at least one
    contact validated urgently.
 ▪ NRI accounts — given extended timeline (until April 30, 2025) to complete validation for transacting with
   new intermediaries.

8. Frequently Asked Questions
Q: Is KRA Validation the same as doing KYC again?
A: No. KRA Validation is not a fresh KYC. It is simply confirming that the contact details (mobile + email)
already registered on your KYC record are still active and belong to you. It takes 5 minutes online and
requires no new documents — just OTP access to the registered mobile and email.
Q: My investor's existing SIP is running fine. Do they still need to validate?
A: If the investor's KYC status is 'KYC Registered', existing SIPs and redemptions CONTINUE without
interruption. However, if they want to start a new SIP with an AMC they've never transacted with before,
they will need to validate first. It is best practice to complete validation proactively to avoid any future
disruption.

Q: What if the investor doesn't have access to the old mobile number registered on KYC?
A: This is a KYC Modification case — not just validation. The investor must first update the KYC record to the
new mobile number through the broker/intermediary (takes up to 72 hours), and then complete the OTP
validation. Refer to P09_KYC_Mobile_Email_Update.docx.
Q: Can the investor validate at any KRA, or must it be the KRA where they are registered?
A: The investor must validate on the KRA where their KYC record is held. Check CVL KRA first. If not found
there, check CAMS, NDML, and DotEx KRAs. Once the correct KRA is identified, do the OTP validation on that
KRA's portal.
Q: Does KRA Validation expire? Does it need to be done periodically?
A: Once the status reaches 'KYC Validated', it remains valid until SEBI introduces any new regulatory changes.
There is currently no periodic re-validation requirement. However, if the investor changes their mobile/email,
they must update and re-validate.
Q: What about NRI investors — do they also need to validate?
A: Yes, NRI investors also need 'KYC Validated' status for full transacting rights. However, the June 2024
amendment gave NRI investors an extended deadline of April 30, 2025 to validate for new intermediary
transactions. NRI KYC must reflect the correct NRI status — see P04_RI_to_NRI_Conversion.docx for the RI-
to-NRI KYC update process.
Q: What is PAN–Aadhaar linking and why is it needed for KYC Validated status?
A: SEBI requires that investor PAN be linked to their Aadhaar on the Income Tax portal as a prerequisite for
'KYC Validated' status. This linking can be done at https://www.incometax.gov.in — under E-File → Income
Tax Returns → Link Aadhaar. Without this linkage, even after OTP validation, the KYC status may remain 'KYC
Registered'.

  Q4. What does each KYC status mean, and how does it affect mutual fund
  transactions?
  KYC Status Types & Their Transaction Impact

As per SEBI's KYC framework (updated April–June 2024), every mutual fund investor's KYC record is classified
into one of five statuses by the KYC Registration Agency (KRA). The status directly determines which mutual

fund transactions the investor is permitted to carry out. This document explains each status clearly and
shows the exact transaction impact.

 Status                                At a Glance                             Transactions

 ✅ KYC VALIDATED                       Fully compliant — all 5 checks          ALL transactions allowed
                                       passed

 ⚠️ KYC REGISTERED                     Partially verified — contact OTP not    Existing AMC: All OK | New AMC:
                                       done or non-Aadhaar OVD                 Restricted

 ❌ KYC ON-HOLD                         Non-compliant — address/contacts        ALL transactions BLOCKED including
                                       unverified                              redemption

 🚫 KYC REJECTED                        KYC application was rejected by the     ALL transactions BLOCKED
                                       KRA

 ➖ KYC NOT FOUND                       No KYC record exists for this PAN on    Cannot invest — fresh KYC required

Detailed Explanation — Each Status
 KYC VALIDATED 📌 What it means: The investor's KYC record has been independently verified by the KRA
 against all official databases. All 5 validation points are confirmed: PAN is active, name & DOB match, address is
 validated, and BOTH mobile and email have been OTP-verified by the investor. This is the highest level of KYC
 compliance.
 🔍 Caused by: KYC was done using Aadhaar (online or physical), contact details are current and OTP-verified
 at the KRA portal, and PAN is active. Most investors who completed KYC via Video KYC / Aadhaar OTP after
 2022 will have this status.
 ✅ MF Transactions ALLOWED: ALL transactions without any restriction — new purchases, SIP registrations,
 redemptions, switches, SWP, STP, dividend options, NFO subscriptions, investing with any new AMC for the
 first time.
 ❌ MF Transactions BLOCKED: Nothing blocked — full transaction freedom.
 🔧 How to fix / upgrade: No action required. This is the ideal status.

 ⚠️ KYC REGISTERED (also shown as 'KYC Verified')
 📌 What it means: The investor completed KYC and basic details (PAN, name, DOB) are verified, but the KRA
 could not fully validate the record. This happens when KYC was done using non-Aadhaar documents (passport,
 driving licence, voter ID), or when the mobile/email OTP confirmation at the KRA portal has not yet been
 completed. As of the May 2024 SEBI amendment, PAN–Aadhaar non-linkage alone does NOT push an investor
 to On-Hold — they remain at Registered.
 🔍 Caused by: KYC done before 2022 using non-Aadhaar OVDs (passport, DL, voter ID); or KYC done with
 Aadhaar but mobile/email OTP not yet confirmed at KRA portal; or only ONE of the two contacts (mobile or
 email) has been OTP-verified.
 ✅ MF Transactions ALLOWED: Existing SIPs continue uninterrupted. All transactions (purchase, redemption,

switch, SWP, STP) with AMCs the investor has ALREADY transacted with — ALLOWED. Redemptions from
existing folios — ALLOWED.
❌ MF Transactions BLOCKED: New investments / SIP registrations with an AMC the investor has NEVER
transacted with before — BLOCKED until KYC is upgraded to Validated OR investor submits fresh Aadhaar-
based KYC to that specific AMC. NFO subscriptions with new fund houses — restricted.
🔧 How to fix / upgrade: Complete mobile + email OTP validation at the KRA portal (P09). Status upgrades to
KYC Validated within 24 hours. No new documents needed — just OTP access to the registered contacts.

KYC ON-HOLD 📌 What it means: The KYC record is non-compliant with SEBI requirements. The KRA has
placed the record on hold because critical validation has failed — either the address could not be verified, or
BOTH mobile and email are unverified at the KRA. This is a serious status — ALL mutual fund transactions are
frozen until the issue is resolved. Even existing SIPs and redemptions are blocked.
🔍 Caused by: Both mobile AND email are unverified at the KRA (neither OTP-confirmed); or address proof on
KYC is not acceptable/verifiable; or KYC was originally done with documents that are no longer accepted as
OVDs (e.g., utility bills, bank statements used as address proof in old KYC); or KYC was based on documents
that cannot be matched with government databases.
✅ MF Transactions ALLOWED: NOTHING. All transactions are blocked including: new purchases, existing SIP
continuations, redemptions of existing units, switches, SWP, STP. The investor cannot access their own money
until the hold is resolved.
❌ MF Transactions BLOCKED: ALL transactions — purchases, redemptions, SIPs (new and running), switches,
SWP, STP, dividend payout requests. CRITICAL: Even redemption of existing holdings is blocked.
🔧 How to fix / upgrade: URGENT: Investor must complete fresh KYC with a valid Aadhaar through a SEBI-
registered intermediary (broker, AMC, distributor). For new investors: use Investwell Video KYC (P07). For
existing investors: KYC Modification Form + Aadhaar at nearest AMC/RTA. Once fresh KYC is approved, do OTP
validation on KRA portal (P09).

KYC REJECTED 📌 What it means: The KYC application was reviewed and formally rejected by the KRA. This is
different from On-Hold — a Rejected status means the KRA has actively declined the KYC record, usually due to
document fraud, significant mismatches in identity details, or regulatory non-compliance.
🔍 Caused by: Significant mismatch between submitted documents and PAN/Aadhaar database; forged or
tampered identity documents; identity details (name, DOB) on documents don't match each other; regulatory
flagging by SEBI / law enforcement; or submission of expired documents.
✅ MF Transactions ALLOWED: Nothing. All MF transactions blocked — same impact as On-Hold.
❌ MF Transactions BLOCKED: ALL transactions — purchases, redemptions, SIPs, switches, SWP, STP. The
investor cannot transact in any mutual fund scheme.
🔧 How to fix / upgrade: Identify the reason for rejection (contact the KRA directly). Once resolved, the
investor must submit fresh KYC with corrected, valid Aadhaar-based documents through a SEBI-registered
intermediary. If documents are fine, escalate to the KRA or AMC for clarification.

➖ KYC NOT FOUND (No Record / Not Registered)
📌 What it means: No KYC record exists for the investor's PAN on any of the four KRAs (CVL, CAMS, NDML,

 DotEx). The investor has never completed KYC, or the KYC done was on a different PAN.
 🔍 Caused by: First-time investor who has never done MF KYC; investor used a different PAN for KYC than the
 one being checked; KYC record was deleted or purged for some reason (very rare).
 ✅ MF Transactions ALLOWED: None — the investor cannot invest in any mutual fund without first
 completing KYC.
 ❌ MF Transactions BLOCKED: ALL transactions. No folio can be opened, no investment accepted.
 🔧 How to fix / upgrade: Complete fresh KYC through the Investwell Video KYC process (P07). Once KYC is
 submitted and approved by the KRA, the investor can begin investing. The status will show as 'KYC Validated' if
 done via Aadhaar eKYC.

Special Cases & Exceptions
HUF (Hindu Undivided Family) Investors
Even if the HUF's KYC is not 'Validated', existing orders, running SIPs, and redemptions are NOT blocked. HUF
investors are given special treatment under the SEBI 2024 framework. Only new investments with new AMCs
require Validated status.
NRI Investors (Non-Resident Indians)
NRI investors with 'KYC Registered' status were given an extended deadline of April 30, 2025 to complete
validation before restrictions apply to new AMC investments. NRI KYC must also reflect the correct tax status
(NRI, not RI) — see P04 for RI-to-NRI conversion.
Minor Investors (below 18 years)
KYC is registered in the name of the guardian. When the minor turns 18, the folio must be converted to an
independent adult account — fresh KYC in the investor's own name is required at that point.
PAN–Aadhaar Not Linked
As per SEBI's May 14, 2024 amendment, PAN–Aadhaar non-linkage no longer causes a KYC On-Hold status on
its own. Investors remain at 'KYC Registered' and can continue transacting with existing AMCs. However, full
'KYC Validated' status ultimately requires Aadhaar linkage.

Inoperative PAN
If an investor's PAN has been rendered inoperative by the Income Tax Department (due to non-linkage with
Aadhaar after the IT department deadline), mutual fund transactions may be blocked at the AMC level,
separate from KYC status. Investor must reactivate PAN by paying the penalty on the IT portal.

KYC MODIFICATION — How to Update a New Mobile Number or Email ID
If an investor needs to CHANGE their mobile number or email ID to a new one (i.e., the old contact is no
longer accessible), they cannot do this directly on the KRA portals — it must go through their broker or
mutual fund distributor. Here is the end-to-end process:
Step 1: Investor Submits Change Request to Broker / Distributor
 ▪ The investor contacts their broker or MF distributor and requests a KYC contact update.
 ▪ They provide: old PAN, new mobile number, new email ID, and supporting identity proof.
 ▪ This can usually be done via the broker's website (Investor Login → Profile → Modify KYC) or by
   submitting a physical KYC Modification Form.
Step 2: Broker Submits Modification Request to KRA
 ▪ The broker/distributor submits a KYC Modification Request to the relevant KRA (CVL, CAMS, NDML, or
   DotEx — whichever holds the investor's KYC record).
 ▪ The request includes the investor's PAN, old and new contact details, and verification documents.
Step 3: KRA Processes the Update (within 72 hours)
 ▪ The KRA reviews and processes the modification request.
 ▪ Processing typically takes up to 72 working hours (3 business days).
 ▪ The investor receives an SMS/email notification once the new contact has been updated on the KRA
   record.
Step 4: Investor Validates New Contact via KRA Portal (OTP Confirmation)
 ▪ Once updated, the investor must VALIDATE the new contact at the KRA portal.
 ▪ They follow the same OTP validation process described above for their KRA.
 ▪ This final step moves the KYC status to 'KYC Validated'.
 Successive KYC modification requests can only be placed after 7–15 business days from the previous
 modification (waiting period varies by KRA). Plan accordingly if multiple updates are needed.

Important Points to Remember
 ▪ KYC is PAN-based — an investor's record lives on exactly ONE KRA (whichever first registered them).
   Check CVL KRA first; if not found there, check the others.
 ▪ After April 1, 2024 (SEBI circular): ‘KYC Verified’ investors must validate email + mobile on the KRA portal
   to reach ‘KYC Validated’ status. Until then, new MF investments may be restricted.
 ▪ Existing investments and SIPs are NOT affected for ‘KYC Verified’ investors — only NEW investments
   require the upgrade.
 ▪ ‘KYC On-Hold’ investors must complete full fresh KYC with PAN + Aadhaar linkage before any
    transactions.
 ▪ Both mobile AND email must be verified in the same session on the KRA portal — validating only one is
   insufficient for status upgrade.
 ▪ OTPs are valid for a short time (typically 5 minutes) — ensure the investor has access to their registered
   mobile and email BEFORE starting the process.
 ▪ For investors whose registered mobile/email is no longer active: the Modification process (through
   broker) must be completed FIRST, then followed by the OTP validation on the KRA portal.

 KRA record through the offline route?
 Physical (Offline) KRA Change Request

 What This Document Covers
 This guide explains how an investor can request changes to their existing KRA KYC record through the physical
 (offline) route — that is, by filling a KYC Change Request Form and submitting it at a SEBI-registered
 intermediary (AMC branch, CAMS, KFintech, or authorised distributor).
 The four types of changes covered:
 A. Contact Details — Mobile number and email ID

 For each change type, this document covers: eligibility, required documents, whether In-Person Verification
 (IPV) is needed, the submission process, processing timeline, and important post-change steps.

  Q7. What is CKYC, how does an investor obtain a CKYC number, and how does it
  differ from KRA KYC?
  CKYC (Central KYC) Explained

  📋 In one line: CKYC (Central KYC) is a single, universal KYC that works across ALL financial institutions in India
  — banks, mutual funds, insurance, and pension. Once done, you get a 14-digit CKYC number (called KIN) and
  never need to re-submit KYC documents to any bank, AMC, or insurer again. It is managed by CERSAI, a
  government body under the Ministry of Finance.

1. What is CKYC?
CKYC stands for Central Know Your Customer. It is India's centralised KYC registry — a single government-
maintained database that stores the KYC records of all financial sector customers. It was launched in
February 2017 under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002.
Before CKYC, an investor had to complete separate KYC formalities with each institution they approached —
one for the bank, one for the mutual fund, one for insurance, and so on. CKYC eliminated this by creating one
centralised identity record, usable everywhere.

6. How CKYC and KRA KYC Work Together — The Big Picture
These two systems are not competing — they are layered on top of each other. Here is how they interact
when an investor does KYC:
1. Investor completes KYC — Via broker/AMC (physical form), or Video KYC (Aadhaar OTP + eSign), or
visiting a bank branch.
2. Intermediary uploads to KRA — The broker/AMC/bank uploads the investor's KYC record to the relevant
KRA (CVL, CAMS, NDML, or DotEx). The investor's MF KYC is now live — they can start investing.
3. KRA uploads to CKYC Registry — As mandated by SEBI, the KRA forwards the KYC record to the CERSAI
CKYC Registry. CERSAI processes and validates it against IT/Aadhaar databases.
4. CERSAI issues 14-digit KIN — CERSAI creates a CKYC record and issues the 14-digit KIN to the investor via
SMS/email. The investor now has BOTH a KRA KYC record AND a CKYC number.
5. Investor uses KIN for future accounts — When the investor opens a bank account, buys insurance, or
invests with a new AMC — they just provide the 14-digit KIN. The institution fetches the full KYC record from
CERSAI — no new documents needed.

Timestamp 0:00 · jump to this point in the video
The Investor Data Mining Report is a flexible query tool. Choose asset class, report type (Portfolio Valuation
or Capital Gain Unrealized) and grouping, then apply filters — holding days, ARN, change-of-broker AUM, SIP
AUM, user, fund, category and sub-category — to extract a precise slice of your client base. It is used to
answer ad-hoc questions, build target lists and export data (on screen or Excel) for campaigns and reviews.

Risk Profiling (Submitted Risk Profiles overview, Setup Risk Profiler)
Timestamp 5:31 · jump to this point in the video
Risk Profiling has two parts. The Submissions screen reviews all submitted risk profiles — pending/expired
versus submitted — with each client's submission date, RM, AUM, computed risk category (e.g. Moderately
Conservative) and validity. The Setup Risk Profiler screen lets you define the questionnaire itself: the
questions, the answer options and their scores, and the score bands that map to each risk category
(Moderately Conservative, Moderate, Moderately Aggressive, Aggressive).

Billing (billing functionality, generating & managing invoices)
Timestamp 7:41 · jump to this point in the video

Billing covers raising and managing client invoices. From the Billing menu (Create Client Bill and Client Bill
Register) advisors can generate invoices for advisory/transaction fees and track them in a register — useful
for fee-based RIA practices.
